My AI-Powered Design Workflow

From voice note to final design in 4 steps: how I use AI to work smarter without losing the craft.

I used to spend hours on briefs, planning, and file setup before I ever opened Figma. Now my workflow takes me from raw idea to final design in 4 focused steps, each one powered by the right AI tool at the right moment.

Here's exactly how it works.

Step 1 — Voice →︎ Structured brief

Wispr Flow
AI-powered voice-to-text tool

I do a 2–3 minute voice brain dump. Wispr Flow captures everything and sends it straight to Notion. No typing, no formatting, no friction. Just talk and it's documented: raw ideas, constraints, references, and all.

This is the part most designers skip. They jump straight into Figma with a half-formed brief in their head. Getting it out of your head and into words, even messy ones, sets the whole project up better.

Step 2 — Brief →︎ Tasks →︎ Daily focus

Notion AI
AI assistant built into Notion

Notion AI then breaks the brain dump into small, specific tasks. Everything lives in one place: tasks, notes, project docs, useful links, even daily todos. No switching between apps, no scattered files.

The biggest productivity win here isn't the AI. It's the consolidation. Having one source of truth means I can stay focused without context-switching overhead.

Step 3 — Layout →︎ Final design

Figma + Figma Make
Design tool with built-in AI generation

Figma Make generates initial layouts quickly. I import my own design system so AI-generated drafts match my brand's components from the start. Then I use First Draft to explore a wider range of design possibilities before committing to one direction.

After that it's back to traditional Figma: typography, spacing, alignment. All the details that make it feel polished. The AI handles quantity, I handle quality.

Step 4 — Design →︎ Critique

Claude
AI assistant with vision capabilities

Before calling it done, I screenshot and drop it into Claude. It analyses layout pros and cons, identifies ambiguous language, and reduces wordiness so the message is as sharp as the visuals.

This is the step that most surprised me. Having an outside perspective, even an AI one, catches things I've gone blind to after staring at the same design for hours.

The bigger picture

AI handles the scaffolding. I handle the craft.

AI can't do this without designers. But designers can do so much more with it. The key is knowing which decisions to delegate and which ones to keep.

Every tool in this workflow handles a specific kind of friction: capturing, organising, generating, reviewing. None of them replace the design thinking. They just clear the path so more of my time goes toward the work that actually matters.

What does your AI workflow look like? I'd love to hear what's working for you.
