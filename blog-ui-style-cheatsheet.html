Talking about visual direction is notoriously slippery. "Clean" means different things to a fintech designer and a Figma designer. "Dark" could mean Vercel or Spotify or Warp — three completely different things.

This is a reference sheet. Each entry gives you the aesthetic's defining characteristics in prompt-ready language — the exact kind of description you'd paste into v0, give a developer, or use to align in a crit. And a canonical URL to point at when words fail.

01 — Stark Black & White Precision

Stark Black & White Precision vercel.com ↗

Absolute restraint. Every element earns its place or gets removed. Negative space is the primary design tool, not a byproduct of good layout.

Pure black background (#000 or #0a0a0a), white body text, zero gradients, zero illustration. Tight letter-spacing on headings. Inter or Geist typeface. Grid-perfect layout with generous white space. No decorative elements. If it doesn't carry information, it doesn't exist.
Screenshot — vercel.com

02 — Purple Gradient Prestige

Purple Gradient Prestige stripe.com ↗

Feels expensive without being loud. The gradient does the emotional work. Typography pulls back — weight 300, generous line-height, nothing competing with the depth of the background.

Deep indigo-to-periwinkle gradient background (e.g. #1a0533 → #6b3fa0 → #a78bfa). Font weight 300–400, large display text at tight tracking. Floating card elements with soft glow or shadow. Smooth layered depth. Clean sans-serif (Söhne, Haas Grot, or similar). Premium feel without ornamentation.
Screenshot — stripe.com

03 — Dark + One Vivid Accent

Dark + One Vivid Accent supabase.com ↗

Near-black surfaces, everything neutral — until the accent appears. The single saturated color hits harder precisely because nothing else is competing with it. Restraint as amplification.

Very dark background (#0a0a0a to #1a1a1a). All UI chrome in neutral greys. One accent color — saturated, pure — used only for CTAs, key metrics, active states, and hover highlights. Zero gradient on the accent. Body text at 80% white. Inter or similar geometric sans.
Screenshot — supabase.com

04 — Warm Editorial Minimalism

Warm Editorial Minimalism notion.so ↗

Feels like a well-designed book, not a dashboard. Off-white surfaces, warm neutrals, generous line height. The content is the visual — decoration would be a distraction.

Off-white background (#F7F6F3 or similar warm cream). Mixed-weight sans or display serif for headings. Body text at 16–18px, Poppins Light or equivalent, 1.8× line height. Soft box shadows. Rounded corners (12–20px). Muted tertiary palette — no saturated colours. Feels calm, readable, human.
Screenshot — notion.so

05 — Cinematic Full-Bleed

Cinematic Full-Bleed apple.com ↗

The photography does the work. Type is an annotation, not a headline. Scrolling is the interaction — every viewport is a scene. Product as protagonist.

Full-viewport imagery or video, no visible grid. Text appears sparse, large, centered over photography — often white or very light. SF Pro, Neue Haas Grotesk, or Universal Sans. Ultra-wide horizontal compositions. Tight headline tracking (−3 to −4px at large sizes). No visible UI chrome on hero sections. Let the image breathe.
Screenshot — apple.com

06 — Playful Multi-Color Brand

Playful Multi-Color Brand figma.com ↗

Four or five brand colours used simultaneously — but with enough structure that they don't fight each other. Energy over precision. Feels engineered but approachable.

Multi-hue palette used in harmony (4–5 colors, no single dominant). Rounded UI — 16–24px border radius. Illustration or icon accents at key touchpoints. Friendly display typeface or bold geometric sans. White or very light backgrounds to let colour pop. Avoid dark mode unless secondary. High visual energy, low formality.
Screenshot — figma.com

07 — Terminal-Native Dark

Terminal-Native Dark warp.dev ↗

Designed for people who live in terminals. Monospace and sans coexist. Panel-based, block-based layout. The aesthetic signals: this tool respects your intelligence and your workflow.

True dark background (#0d0d0d to #1a1a24). Monospace font mixed with geometric sans for UI chrome. Syntax-colored accents (cyan, green, amber, pink) used sparingly for code and highlights. Panel-style or block-command UI. Dense but not cluttered. Minimal marketing gloss — looks like a product screenshot, not a landing page.
Screenshot — warp.dev

08 — Fintech Clarity

Fintech Clarity wise.com ↗

High information density that never feels cluttered. A single bright accent on clean white. Trust is built through layout: visible numbers, clear labels, no dark patterns.

White or very light background. Single accent (bright green, blue, or teal) for CTAs and key actions only. Body type at 14–16px, regular weight, 1.7× line height. Dense tables and data displays with subtle row dividers. No illustration where numbers suffice. Radiates reliability — functional beauty, nothing decorative.
Screenshot — wise.com

How to use this

Copy the prompt language directly into v0, Framer AI, or any generative UI tool. Use the site links as visual reference when briefing a developer or presenting a direction. The canonical examples were chosen because they're consistent — no campaigns, no one-off pages, just their core product or marketing site done well and kept current.

The boundaries between styles blur intentionally. Vercel occasionally uses gradient accents. Stripe has editorial sections. Real products mix fluently — which is exactly why it's useful to name the underlying aesthetics precisely rather than reaching for vague words like "clean" or "modern."
